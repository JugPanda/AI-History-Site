What actually moved the field

Four forces that kept reappearing in every era

Algorithms

New representations changed what problems were learnable.

Logic, perceptrons, backpropagation, convolution, attention, and self-supervision each changed the game differently.

Compute

Hardware turned old ideas into practical systems.

Many ideas existed years before they mattered. Better hardware let them finally work at useful scale.

Data

Benchmarks and corpora made progress visible.

From expert-crafted rules to giant scraped datasets, the field kept moving toward learnable structure in data.

Interfaces

People care when AI changes the surface of work.

ELIZA, Deep Blue, Watson, ChatGPT, and Copilot mattered because the public could feel the shift directly.

Why the story keeps repeating

AI history is not a straight climb. It is a cycle of new representations, new interfaces, overconfidence, and correction.

That is why the field feels both old and shockingly new. The underlying arguments keep resurfacing, but each time with better hardware, bigger datasets, and more immediate contact with everyday work.

Representation

How should intelligence be expressed?

Rules, probabilities, learned features, and attention were not cosmetic shifts. Each changed what could be modeled at all.

Scale

When does more compute actually matter?

Many "new" breakthroughs were old ideas that only became useful once hardware and data reached the right threshold.

Contact

When does the public finally feel it?

ELIZA, Deep Blue, Watson, ChatGPT, and Copilot mattered because they changed the surface where people meet computation.
